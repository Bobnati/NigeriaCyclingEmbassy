REAR LIGHTS As necessary as the rear headlight is, using the back light is surprisingly considered optional by some cyclists. Here is why you need rear lights: As a cyclist, you should move in the direction of traffic.

Which means that you should be moving in the direction that other cars are moving in. Keep in mind that the back light is meant to be reflective and make you noticeable to a driver, letting them know you're there before they get close, particularly if a reflector is fastened to it.

A driver might not see you in a dark or foggy conditions, such as at night and during rainy times and that can cause unwanted incidents. 't safe you. Therefore, it is as important to install rear lights on your bike.

Asides from the fact that rear lights makes you visible increases your safety by making other road users aware of your presence, it also projects the qualities of your bike, increase your confidence when riding at night and in different weather and visibility conditions and make your bike more presentable.
